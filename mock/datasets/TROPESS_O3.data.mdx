    <p>
      ## Introduction
      <p style={{ marginBottom: '0.4em' }}>
      Ozone (O₃) is a key trace gas in the Earth's atmosphere, particularly in the stratosphere, where it protects life by absorbing harmful ultraviolet radiation. However, at ground level, ozone is a significant air pollutant associated with respiratory problems and environmental degradation. Monitoring ozone concentrations is essential for understanding air quality, climate change, and atmospheric chemistry.
      </p>
      <p style={{ marginBottom: '0.4em' }}>
      The TROPESS Chemical Reanalysis Ozone Monthly 3D Product provides global, monthly gridded estimates of ozone concentration (in ppb) from 2005 to 2021 at multiple pressure levels between 1000 and 60 hPa. Developed using advanced data assimilation techniques, this dataset integrates satellite observations and chemical transport models to produce reliable ozone concentration estimates.
      </p>
    </p>
  </Prose>
</Block>

<Block style={{justifyContent: 'center', alignItems: 'center', margin: '0 auto', width: '100%' }}>
   <Prose style={{ textAlign: 'justify', display: 'flex', alignItems: 'center' }}>
      <p>
    ## Data Preparation
        The dataset was developed through rigorous data assimilation using satellite measurements such as ozone column retrievals from the Ozone Monitoring Instrument (OMI) and the Tropospheric Monitoring Instrument (TROPOMI). The integration of these observations with chemical models ensures the highest possible accuracy in ozone concentration estimates.
    </p>
  </Prose>
</Block>

    ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
    <p style={{ marginBottom: '0.9em' }}>
      <strong>What is ozone?</strong><br />
      Ozone (O₃) is a gas composed of three oxygen atoms. It plays a critical role in protecting life by absorbing ultraviolet radiation in the stratosphere, but at ground level, it contributes to air pollution and health issues.
    </p>
    <p style={{ marginBottom: '0.9em' }}>
      <strong>How is the data reported?</strong><br />
      The dataset provides ozone concentrations in ppb (parts per billion), which represents the amount of ozone in the air.
    </p>
    <p style={{ marginBottom: '0.9em' }}>
      <strong>How can I use this dataset?</strong><br />
      The data can be used for environmental monitoring, air quality studies, climate research, and regulatory assessments concerning ozone pollution.
    </p>
    <p style={{ marginBottom: '0.9em' }}>
      <strong>What tools are available for data analysis?</strong><br />
